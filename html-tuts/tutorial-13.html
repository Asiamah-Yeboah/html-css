What are Emojis in HTML?

Emojis are actually Unicode characters, just like the letters and numbers you type every day! They're part of the Unicode Standard, which is a universal character encoding system.

Each emoji has a unique Unicode code point, just like every letter has a unique ASCII code!

The great news is that modern browsers automatically support emojis as long as you're using UTF-8 encoding (which you should be!). ‚úÖ

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Emoji Mistakes Beginners Make

  • Overusing emojis: Makes content look unprofessional
  • Forgetting accessibility: Screen readers announce emoji names
  • Using platform-specific emojis: They look different across devices
  • Not testing on different devices: Emoji display varies
  • Using emojis in important navigation: Can confuse users
  • Ignoring cultural differences: Emojis have different meanings worldwide

Cultural Awareness

The "thumbs up" üëç means approval in most Western cultures but is offensive in parts of the Middle East. The "OK" üëå hand gesture is offensive in Brazil. Research emoji meanings for your target audience!
